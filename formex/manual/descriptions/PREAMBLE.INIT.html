PREAMBLE.INIT

[element]

Introductory phrase of the preamble

The PREAMBLE.INIT element is used to mark up the introductory phrase of the preamble, which often consists of a list of authors in their full name.

For example:

  • Commission: THE COMMISSION OF THE EUROPEAN COMMUNITIES
  • Council: THE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION
  • Court of Justice: THE COURT OF JUSTICE
  • Representatives of the governments of the Member States: THE REPRESENTATIVES OF THE GOVERNMENTS OF THE MEMBER STATES OF THE EUROPEAN COAL AND STEEL COMMUNITY, MEETING IN THE COUNCIL
  • etc.

General rules

Element

If the introduction consists of several paragraphs, each of them must be marked up with a separate P element.

Example

<PREAMBLE>
  <PREAMBLE.INIT>THE COUNCIL OF THE EUROPEAN UNION,</PREAMBLE.INIT>
  <GR.VISA>
    <VISA>Having regard to the Treaty establishing the European Community and in particular Article 95 thereof,</VISA>
  </GR.VISA>
  <GR.CONSID>  </GR.CONSID>
 </PREAMBLE>
